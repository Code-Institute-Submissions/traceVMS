Contractors:

All contractors carrying out work on this site shall take every precaution to reduce, as far as reasonabily practical, the risk of food contamination and cross contamination of allergens used on site. Every precaution shall be taken to prevent injury to others working in, or passing through, the immediate area.

It is the responsibility of all contractors working on site to ensure the following:

  1. Surrounding areas of plant must be protected from contamination as necessary.
  2. Clean protective clothing must be worn at all times; if this becomes dirty ask your site contact for a duplicate set.
  3. Always use clean tools, clean them after use and before moving to a new production line or factory area. This is to minimise any cross contamination risks.
  4. On completion of work
  • Ensure all potential foreign bodies have been cleared from the area e.g. nuts,bolts,cloths,swarf,etc
  • The area must be thoroughly cleaned down.
  • All tools must be accounted for and removed.
  • All panels and covers must be replaced.

Any areas worked on must be inspected by your site contact before they can sign off the work and before any production can commence

Permit to Work:

A permit to work system is in place. All contractors are required to complete “Site Entry Permit” prior to commencing any work on site. Where a work activity involves tasks such as working at height, hot work or entry into a confined space, additional permits will also be required. You must not leave site without signing off all PTWs once the work is complete.

Waste Disposal:

Contractors are responsible for ensuring that any waste they generate is removed from the site and disposed of in accordance with the Environmental Protection Act (Duty of Care Regulations 1991). Waste must not be unlawfully disposed of, it must be stored appropriately to avoid theft or vandalism, it must only be transferred to an authorised person and must be accompanied by adequate documentation.
